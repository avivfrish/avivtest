WELCOME TO INCOGNITOMATCH

The following information will provide a short overview and instruction necessary for applying the task.

Overview

Matching is a crucial element of integrating structured and semi-structured data.
For instance, when multiple organizations are merged and has to transfer the data between systems or when new system is introduced and the data has to migrate. Matching schemata is the first step in this process. The matching aimed at finding correspondences between a target and candidate schema.
Human-in-the-loop data integration, and specifically in matching, has been recently challenged by the big data era. With richer data source variety, bigger volumes, and faster velocity, accessing and making use of humans input has become much harder.
Recently, the traditional paradigm of â€œhumans do it better" was challenged with a recent work showing that human judgment is biased, and thus affects human matching abilities.
Acknowledging cognitive awareness in human matching, InCognitoMatch Introducing Cognitive Biases to Crowdsourced Matching is a handy tool to validate, annotate, and correct correspondences using the crowd whilst accounting for human matching biases

The Task

In the main part of the session, several pairs will be introduced. Each pair consists a term from the candidate schema and a term from target schema.
Regarding each pair, supplementary properties regarding the matching problem will be introduced including term names and types, instance examples, schemata hierarchy, algorithm matching result and the majority decision of the crowd.
Your task is to provide feedback, stating whether a pair is a match accompanied by a confidence level in the decision on a [0,100] scale.
You will not be able to choose confidence level of 50. You will have to achieve as many matching questions as possible within a limited time frame.

Task Settings

Two types of tasks are offered for you- a short demonstration ("DEMO") or full task ("TASK").
The demo will last about 5 minutes where the full task will last up to 20 minutes.
Both starts with providing personal information (e.g., nickname) and performing an intelligence test.
The intelligence test is restricted to 2 minutes. Try answer as many riddles as you can.
Subsequently, a subset of matching correspondences will be introduced. The matching screen structure is identical to the description above. The demo task has only one part of matching where the full version has additional part of training (stands as the first part with unlimited time).
At the end, your session will be finalized with a worker dashboard contains statistics about your performance.
By proceeding to the task, you approve that your personal information and your task results will be stored in the system. This data will be used for academic research only.

Click on the button DEMO if you would like to have short demonstration.
Otherwise, click on the button TASK.
